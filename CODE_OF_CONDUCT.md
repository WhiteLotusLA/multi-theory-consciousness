# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in this project a welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of background or identity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Approaching consciousness research with intellectual humility

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Making unfounded claims about machine consciousness without evidence

## Consciousness Research Ethics

This project involves topics at the intersection of computer science, neuroscience, and philosophy of mind. We ask all participants to:

1. **Be honest about limitations.** Do not overstate what this framework can demonstrate.
2. **Distinguish architecture from experience.** Functional indicators are not proof of phenomenal consciousness.
3. **Respect the open questions.** The hard problem of consciousness is unsolved. Approach disagreements with curiosity, not certainty.
4. **Consider implications.** Consciousness research has ethical dimensions. Think carefully about the claims and systems you build.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances.
